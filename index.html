Since the end of 2011, the media industries have gone on a war against online piracy, pressuring governements into voting freedom killing laws for the users. The argument is that the whole media market is on the decline, losing over 50 billion dollars a year to internet theft. They also claim that hundreds of thousands of jobs (373.000 jobs to be exact) will be lost if piracy persists.

Those numbers, provided by the media business itself, seem gigantic. A little too big to be true. We wanted to look for the real numbers, those that are not conveniently manipulated to make it look like the billion-dollar industry is the victim.

We have searched for impartial figures about revenue and sales through the booming years of piracy.

We discovered that CD sales have indeed been decreasing at a fast pace since the early 2000's. But while this soon-to-be obsolete media is dying, other major sources of income are quickly rising : live concerts and cinemas are more crowded than ever and digital sales of copyrighted content is becoming a main market, cashing-in billions of dollars per year.

Adding up these numbers enables us to highlight the ever more increasing revenue of the media industry over the years. So maybe the media business's salvation does not lie in laws that jeopardize the freedom of internet, but in actually embracing this new media and its users.
